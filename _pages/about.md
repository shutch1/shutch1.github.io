I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of California, Santa Barbara, advised by Prof. Mahnoosh Alizadeh. My research focuses on safe learning and control, online optimization, bandit learning, pricing design, and smart grid applications.

I received an M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UC Santa Barbara in December 2024 and a B.S. in Electrical Engineering, summa cum laude, from the Colorado School of Mines in June 2021.

My recent work studies algorithms for decision-making under safety constraints, including constrained online convex optimization, safe bandit learning, and safe control. I have also worked on stochastic model predictive control with deep forecasting models for energy systems.
